According to NYC law, it must be at least  inside your apartment.

Over 10,000 people in NYC are without heat. Are you one of them?

If Your landlord has not responded to your requests to turn on the heat, here are the steps you can take:

Contact 311

Call 311 or online. The city should send an investigator to come take a temperature reading in your apartment.

Why?
Contacting 311 will help:
  1. "Encourage" your landlord to turn on your heat. 311 takes your complaint and sends it to the NY Housing Department (HPD). The Housing Department then contacts your landlord and lets them know a case has been filed.
  2. Build Your Case. Your complaints are all catalogued, and can be referred to if you choose to take your landlord to court.
What Next?
Contacting 311 every day that you don't have heat. An inspector should eventually come, although sometimes they don’t, and sometimes they come unannounced.

Document your heat complaints

Keeping track of every day you don't have heat can help you if you end up in court. Use our [Heat Log Tool], or download a PDF from The MetCouncil on Housing to print.

Reach Out for Help

At any time during this process you can contact an organization that can assist you through this process. To get some recommedations, you can call the MetCouncil's tenant help hotline at 212-979-0611.

Try contacting your city council member!

Talk to your neighbors. If your neighbors are also struggling with heat, you should work together to file a case against your landlord. Everyone should be contacting 311, at least every day.
